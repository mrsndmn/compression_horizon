Progressive Cramming: Reliable Token Compression
and What It Reveals
A frozen LM can compress >1000 tokens into one embedding — but perfect reconstruction is brittle steering, not transferable semantics.

1 Progressive Cramming

Optimize per-sample embedding $\mathbf{e}$ so a frozen LM reconstructs tokens. Extend target one token at a time, advance only after perfect convergence:

  • Halt on first failure → sharp capacity boundary
  • 100% greedy generation + honest per-sample capacity
  • Saved $\mathbf{e}$ at each step → optimizer trajectory

5 Attention Knockout
Llama-3.1-8B. Forward (mask early layers) restores downstream accuracy. Reverse fails until early layers are reached.

Early layers cause both reconstruction and capability collapse. Late-layer attention mass (20–60%) is a symptom. Consistent across Llama-3.1-8B, Pythia-1.4B, SmolLM2-1.7B.

6 Why Not Full Cramming?

Full cramming (Kuratov et al., 2025) optimizes all tokens at once. Higher capacity — but residual errors concentrate on early positions, and one early miss cascades through autoregressive generation:

7 Contributions
  • Progressive cramming — 100% generation guarantee with honest per-sample capacity
  • Low-dim trajectories — 30–100 PCA components for 99% variance; solutions near-orthogonal
  • Downstream collapse — HellaSwag/ARC drop, generative MMLU → 0%
  • Causal analysis — early layers causally responsible for collapse
  • Low-dim projection — up to 3.3× capacity boost across model families
✓ Takeaways
  • Brittle Steering — perfect reconstruction stores nothing the model can use; downstream benchmarks drop, generative MMLU collapses to ~0%
  • Early Layers — across all model families, the embedding’s causal influence concentrates in the first few layers; late-layer attention mass is a symptom
